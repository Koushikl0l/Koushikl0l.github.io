Abstract

A multimodal approach for predicting Air Quality Index (AQI) by integrating numerical and image data. A precision-integrated numerical branch and an image encoder using EfficientNetB0 extract features. Cross-modal attention is utilized to enhance interaction between modalities, followed by multi-head self-attention and a gated fusion mechanism for robust integration. A contrastive loss function is incorporated during training to align numerical and image embeddings, leading to state-of-the-art performance in AQI regression from multiple data sources.

Keywords: Multimodal learning, AQI regression, contrastive loss, robust fusion

1. Introduction

Predicting AQI is challenging due to the diverse nature of data sources. Traditional methods often fail to capture the complex relationships between environmental sensor data and image features. Our proposed model addresses this by fusing both modalities through attention mechanisms and contrastive alignment, enhancing accuracy and generalization.

2. Numerical and Image Representations

The numerical branch processes environmental sensor inputs through dense layers, while the image branch leverages EfficientNetB0 to extract high-level features. Both are embedded into vector spaces for cross-modal interaction.

3. Cross-Modal and Self-Attention Mechanisms

Cross-modal attention aligns the numerical and image embeddings by computing query-key interactions. This is followed by multi-head self-attention to allow internal context reasoning across combined embeddings. A gated fusion module selectively merges attended features for final AQI prediction.
